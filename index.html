How Each KPI Is Measured

60% Documentation Time Saved
What is measured Reduction in hours/week each educator spends on lesson planning, portfolio writing, assessment reports, and parent communications
Data sources Structured weekly time diaries (2-week samples at baseline, Month 9, Month 12) cross-referenced with platform usage analytics (timestamped session durations per document type)
Baseline Collected during Months 4–5 across all pilot educators. Expected ~8 hrs/week based on Harper, Wilson & McGrath-Champ (2025) findings in comparable ECE settings
Calculation ((Baseline hours − Post-intervention hours) / Baseline hours) × 100, per educator, reported as cohort mean with standard deviation
Reporting Dashboard widget (real-time % vs. baseline); detailed per-educator breakdown at Month 5, 9, and 12; included in ECDA Impact Report
100% Differentiated Lessons
What is measured Percentage of AI-generated lesson plans that include scaffolding strategies for 2+ distinct learner profiles (e.g., Kinesthetic, Visual-Spatial, Advanced Verbal)
Data sources Platform lesson plan metadata (structured fields for linked profiles and scaffolding strategies) with automated validation; monthly 10% sample audit by Pedagogical Specialist using a 3-point rubric (profile relevance, strategy actionability, strategy distinctness)
Baseline Pre-platform audit of 20 existing lesson plans per centre during Month 4. Expected 20–40% showing explicit differentiation (OECD TALIS Starting Strong, 2018)
Calculation Plans with 2+ profiles / total plans generated. 100% target is a system design constraint: the AI requires class profiles as input and produces differentiated scaffolding as output
Reporting Real-time compliance rate on Director's Dashboard; monthly pedagogical audit report with qualitative notes; final tally in Month 12 ECDA report
85% IDP Accuracy
What is measured Educator agreement with AI-generated Individual Development Plan recommendations: strongest QTT domain, primary growth area, and recommended SFw-aligned PD pathway
Data sources 5-item Likert-scale validation survey per educator per term (items: strongest domain accuracy, growth area accuracy, PD relevance, coaching focus alignment, willingness to act); director corroboration rating; cross-referenced with platform coaching transcripts and lesson plan patterns
Baseline Educators rate accuracy of their existing manual IDP using the same 5-item instrument during Month 5, establishing a comparison benchmark (typical 70–75% agreement for human-generated PD mappings)
Calculation Percentage of survey items rated 4 ("Agree") or 5 ("Strongly Agree") across all educators. 85% target exceeds typical human-generated benchmarks due to continuous data collection vs. annual snapshots
Reporting Per-term (Month 7 and Month 11); Likert distribution chart; per-educator IDP status on Dashboard; qualitative disagreement notes for AI improvement; included in Month 12 ECDA report
2x Coaching Cycles
What is measured Completed AI coaching sessions per educator per week. A completed session requires 4+ conversational turns and engagement with at least one QTT domain tag
Data sources Platform session logs (session ID, educator ID, mode, timestamps, turn count, QTT domains, completion status). Quality check via average session duration and depth-of-reflection score (Gibbs' Reflective Cycle stages)
Baseline Current structured coaching frequency reported by each centre during Month 4–5 onboarding. Expected ~0.5–1.0 sessions/educator/week based on Singapore ECE norms
Calculation Total completed sessions / active educators per week. "2x" is contextually calibrated to each centre's baseline (e.g., baseline 1/week → target 2/week). AI coaching removes scheduling and availability barriers
Reporting Weekly on Dashboard (real-time counter); monthly trend chart showing frequency over Months 5–12; per-educator breakdown by coaching mode; correlation analysis between coaching frequency and QTT improvement in Month 12 report
12-Month Roadmap

Sandbox Implementation Timeline

A phased approach from development through pilot to national scaling, designed for rigorous evaluation and sustainable impact.

Months 1–3

Development & AI Foundation

Deploy cloud infrastructure on Azure SG region. Build the four core modules (Planner, Portfolio, Coach, Dashboard). Engineer and validate AI prompts for QTT/SFw alignment using GPT-4.1-mini via Azure OpenAI. Implement PDPA compliance framework and Microsoft Entra ID authentication with role-based access control.

Months 4–5

Pilot Deployment & Onboarding

Onboard 2–3 pilot centres. Conduct educator onboarding workshops and digital literacy training. Establish baseline metrics for documentation time, lesson quality, and coaching frequency. Complete security audit and penetration testing.

Months 6–9

Active Classroom Usage & Iteration

Live platform usage across pilot centres. Generate SFw-aligned Individual Development Plans (IDPs). Run weekly AI coaching sessions. Collect iterative feedback for continuous improvement. Refine AI models and user experience based on real-world data.

Months 10–12

Impact Assessment & Scaling

Conduct end-of-pilot QTT assessment. Measure quantitative time-saved metrics against baseline. Administer educator satisfaction surveys. Consolidate findings into comprehensive impact report. Present outcomes and scaling proposal to ECDA. Prepare for national rollout across Busy Bees centres.
