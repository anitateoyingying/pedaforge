Overall Interpretation

Leo’s fire station drawing demonstrates strong creative representation and fine motor control that exceeds typical K1 developmental expectations. The drawing includes a multi-storey structure with identifiable architectural features (door, windows, garage), a fire truck with proportional details (ladder, wheels, red colouring), and evidence of spatial planning. His verbal narration during the activity (“This is where the firemen sleep”) indicates purposeful representation linked to real-world understanding from the SCDF visit.

Aesthetics & Creative Expression

✓
Purposeful use of colour and shape Met

Leo uses red deliberately for the fire truck and differentiates building colours. Shape usage shows intentional architectural representation rather than random marks.

✓
Representational drawing with recognisable forms Met

The fire station, truck, and human figures are all recognisable without explanation. This exceeds the K1 expectation of emerging representational skills.

✓
Planning and composition Met

The drawing shows deliberate spatial arrangement: the building occupies the centre, the truck is positioned in the garage bay, and the ground line anchors the composition.

●
Experimentation with different media Emerging

Drawing uses pencil and coloured pencils only. Consider introducing mixed media (collage, paint, crayon resist) to extend Leo’s creative expression range.

Motor Skills Development

✓
Fine motor control in mark-making Met

Straight lines for the building walls, small details like window panes and ladder rungs demonstrate controlled pencil grip and precision. Above K1 expectations.

●
Letter and number formation Emerging

Leo attempted to label the garage door with “SCDF” but letter formation is inconsistent (S reversed, D partially formed). Continue supporting through meaningful writing contexts.

Recommended Next Steps

  • 1 Introduce 3D construction projects (clay, recyclable materials) to extend Leo’s spatial understanding from 2D drawing into three dimensions.
  • 2 Provide mixed media opportunities (collage, paint, crayon resist) alongside his preferred pencil drawing to expand his creative toolkit.
  • 3 Support letter formation through meaningful contexts (labelling his drawings, creating signs for his constructions) rather than isolated writing practice.
