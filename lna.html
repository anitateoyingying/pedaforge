3 Self-Reflection

Strengths

Strong rapport with families. Confident in creating safe, inclusive environments. Experienced with outdoor learning and nature-based pedagogy.

Areas for Growth

Need to deepen intentional teaching strategies for sustained shared thinking. Want to improve documentation of children’s learning through observation and assessment.

Professional Goals (next 12 months)

Become more confident in using open-ended questioning to extend children’s thinking during play. Develop systematic approaches to documenting learning stories that connect observations to NEL outcomes.

4 AI-Identified Learning Goals

Based on your profile, CWF assessment, and self-reflection, PedaForge has identified the following priority learning goals.

CWF01 Strengthen sustained shared thinking: Develop strategies to extend children’s thinking through open-ended questioning and responsive dialogue during play-based learning.
CWF05 Formative assessment practices: Build confidence in observing, documenting, and interpreting children’s learning to inform curriculum planning and differentiation.
CWF03 Reflective professional practice: Establish a habit of structured self-reflection (using Gibbs’ Cycle) after teaching episodes to identify patterns and plan improvements.

These goals are AI-generated recommendations. You and your centre leaders can review, adjust, and finalise them before they flow into your Individual Development Plan.

5 Individual Development Plan (IDP)

Your IDP is built from the learning goals identified above, mapped to recommended professional development pathways aligned with the Skills Framework for ECCE.

Q1
Sustained Shared Thinking Workshop

Attend centre-based PD on open-ended questioning techniques. Practice with AI Coach (Socratic Inquiry mode) 2x weekly. Document 3 classroom episodes showing SST strategies.

Q2
Observation & Documentation Skills

Complete online module on learning stories (NEL-aligned). Use PedaForge Portfolio to capture 5 observations per week with AI-assisted narrative drafting. Peer review with a colleague.

Q3
Reflective Practice Habit

Establish weekly AI coaching reflections using Gibbs’ Cycle. Share one reflection per month at PLC meeting. Centre leader to observe and provide feedback on one teaching episode.

Q4
Review & Next Cycle

Complete end-of-year self-assessment. Review QTT progress with centre leader. Update CWF proficiency ratings. Set goals for next IDP cycle based on growth trajectory.

This IDP can be exported as a PDF or shared directly with your centre leader for review and endorsement.
